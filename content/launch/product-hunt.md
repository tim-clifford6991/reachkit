## Description (the listing body, ~260 chars + the longer block PH allows)

**Short (feed/preview):**
Building the product and getting it found are different jobs. ReachKit does the
second one. Paste your App Store URL or website and get a Discoverability Score,
a positioning mirror, and a prioritized weekly plan — with evidence and drafts,
not vague advice. Free scan.

**Longer (full description):**
ReachKit is an AI distribution system for solo founders of consumer
subscription products — mobile apps and web SaaS.

You paste one URL. In seconds you watch it work — pulling your reviews, finding
your real competitors, crawling your site. In about two minutes you get:

- A **Discoverability Score** (0–100) that only moves on verified facts, not vibes.
- A **positioning mirror** — what your product *appears* to say it does, in your
  own customers' words. (This one's uncomfortable in a useful way.)
- **Three concrete findings** you can act on today.

Get the full report (free, email-gated) and it expands into an ICP breakdown, a
competitor gap map, and a prioritized action plan across content, outreach, and
SEO/ASO — every action backed by the evidence behind it, an effort estimate, and
an editable draft.

The paid tier ($29/mo Solo) turns it into a weekly operating system: a fresh
action queue every week, intent monitoring, and verification that the work you
did actually went live and moved your score. ($99/mo Growth for more.) But the
scan and the full report are free — start there.

Built by a solo dev who kept stalling at the same wall: shipped the thing,
couldn't get it found. This is the marketing co-founder I couldn't afford.

## First comment (maker's story)

Hi Product Hunt 👋

I'm a solo developer, and ReachKit exists because of a wall I kept hitting.

I'd ship something. It'd get to a few hundred users, or a few hundred dollars in
MRR. And then it would just… stop. Not because the product was bad — because
*getting it found* is a completely different job from building it, and I'm a
builder. Every time, I'd end up with a dozen tabs open — an ASO blog post, a
keyword-tool free trial, a "submit to 100 directories" list, a Reddit thread
full of people confidently disagreeing — and still no actual plan for what to do
on Monday morning.

So I built the co-founder I couldn't hire: paste your App Store URL or your
website, and ReachKit tells you *specifically* why your product is hard to
discover and what to do about it this week.

Two things I tried hard to get right:

1. **Evidence over opinions.** Every finding points at the thing it's reading —
   a specific review, a keyword your competitor ranks for and you don't, a
   comparison page that should exist and doesn't. The score moves only on
   verified components, so it's not a number you can game by feeling good.
2. **Honest data.** The backbone is DataForSEO, Tavily, and official free APIs —
   I deliberately stayed away from the sketchy scraping these tools usually rely
   on. Narrower and defensible beats broad and fragile.

The scan is free. The full report is free (just gated behind an email). There's
a paid tier that turns it into a weekly operating system, but that's not what I
want you to judge today.

Here's my actual ask: **scan your own app or SaaS and tell me if the score is
fair and the findings are true.** If it gets your competitor wrong, or the
positioning mirror misreads you, or a "finding" is obvious — tell me. That
feedback is the most valuable thing I can get out of today.

I'll be in the comments all day. Thank you for taking a look 🙏

## Gallery shot list (in feed order — image 1 is the thumbnail)

> PH shows the first image as the feed thumbnail. Lead with the SCORE + a real
> finding so the value is legible at a glance — never open on a bare logo.
> Suggested 1280×720 (16:9). Use a REAL scanned app (e.g. one of the teardown
> subjects: Opal / Bearable / CardPointers / Sofa / Nudgi) so nothing looks
> mocked. 6 frames; 4–6 is the sweet spot.

1. **Hero — the result, not the brand.** A real Discoverability Score (e.g.
   "34/100") with the radar/breakdown and one punchy finding overlaid. Headline:
   "Here's exactly why your app is hard to find." This is the thumbnail; it has
   to communicate the product in one glance.

2. **The positioning mirror.** Side-by-side: "What your listing says" vs. "What
   your customers say you do" (pulled from reviews). Caption: "The gap is the
   problem." This is the most distinctive screen — show it early.

3. **The three findings.** The free-scan findings card, each finding with its
   evidence link visible. Caption: "Three concrete fixes, each backed by data."

4. **The weekly action plan.** The prioritized action queue from the full
   report — a content card, an outreach card, an SEO/ASO card — each showing
   effort estimate + "view evidence" + "ready-to-edit draft." Caption: "Not
   advice. A plan, with drafts."

5. **Paste-a-URL → working in 10s.** The scan starting: reviews pulled,
   competitors found, site crawled — the "it's actually doing something" moment.
   Caption: "Paste one URL. Watch it work."

6. **The score that moves.** The score over time / verification view — a
   directory marked "live ✓", a keyword that climbed. Caption: "Do the work,
   watch the number move." (Bridges to the paid weekly OS without hard-selling.)

> Optional: a short (≤30s) demo video as the first gallery item often lifts
> conversion — a screen recording of paste-URL → score → one finding. Only if
> it's genuinely smooth; a janky video hurts more than no video.
